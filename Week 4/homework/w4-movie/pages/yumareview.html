REVIEW

3:10 to Yuma directed by James Mangold, and starring Christian Bale, and my all time favorite actor Russel Crowe, explores Mid-Western Arizona when outlaw Ben Wade (Crowe) is captured and must be taken to Yuma to meet his fate. My all time favorite movie, Yuma is not for the fate of heart.

What makes 3:10 to Yuma such an amazing watch is the action, acting and lawlessness that is portrayed. Russel Crowe is such an amazing actor, he fits the role so perfectly. One scene that sticks with me the most is when Ben Wade shows his true colors-- he had murdered a Pinkerton for keeping him awake by singing. Gruesome, but the looks on the surrounding band's faces showed what they were getting into bringing Wade to his demise. Overall, I would rate this movie a 10/10. I truly have a bias here, being a Western lover, but this movie is a great watch.

Trivia

  • Before the final cast was settled, the film was originally going to star Tom Cruise and Eric Bana
  • Director James Mangold required all actors know how to ride a horse for their roles.
  • On the first day of filming, a horse and its rider collided with a camera vehicle.
